The bend

A neuron's weighted sum z = w₁·x₁ + w₂·x₂ + b is school algebra — on its own it can only ever draw a flat plane. The activation y = f(z) is the single thing that bends it into a curve. Switch it and watch the same neuron change shape.

The consequence — can it separate the rings?

A single neuron's boundary is always a straight line, whatever the activation. But stack a whole layer of ReLU neurons and their bends combine — the boundary can now curl right around a ring. Same 300 points, same training. The only difference is the hidden layer.
